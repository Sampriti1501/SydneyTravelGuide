On Foot

Sydney's coastal walks
are world-class.

Sydney's coastline is one of the most dramatic in the world — clifftop paths, hidden beaches, and harbour views that will genuinely stop you in your tracks.

🌊 Bondi to Coogee Coastal Walk
Must Do #1
Distance 6 km
Time 2–3 hrs
Difficulty Easy–Medium
Start Bondi Beach

Sydney's most iconic walk — clifftop paths from Bondi to Coogee, passing five beaches, ocean pools, and dramatic headlands.

The trail hugs dramatic sandstone clifftops from Bondi south to Coogee, passing five distinct beaches: Tamarama (dramatic, compact), Bronte (family favourite with great facilities), Clovelly (sheltered, ideal for snorkelling), and Gordons Bay. Along the way: the Bronte Baths ocean pool carved into clifftop rock, and the hauntingly beautiful Waverley Cemetery above the sea.

💡 Tip: Start at Bondi and walk south to Coogee for the best light. Wear proper shoes — parts have uneven rock surfaces and steps. Buses from Coogee get you back to the city easily.
🌿 Spit Bridge to Manly Walk
Local Favourite
Distance 10 km
Time 3–4 hrs
Difficulty Moderate
Start Spit Bridge

Through native bushland along the harbour — many locals consider this better than Bondi to Coogee. End in Manly and ferry home.

This trail weaves through native Sydney bushland along the harbour's northern shore, constantly shifting between open water, hidden coves, and glimpses of the city skyline. Feels genuinely like a bush hike. Several small secret beaches lie along the route. End in Manly for a meal, then catch the Manly Ferry back for the perfect harbour cruise finale.

💡 Tip: Bus to Spit Bridge, walk to Manly, then ferry home — you get the walk AND the iconic harbour cruise. Carry water; there's only one food stop en route (Clontarf Beach).
🌉 Walk the Harbour Bridge
Closest to Office
Distance 2–4 km
Time 1–2 hrs
Difficulty Easy
Start Milsons Point

Walk across the Harbour Bridge from Milsons Point — steps from the office — completely free. Descend into The Rocks on the city side.

The pedestrian walkway views are unlike any other perspective on Sydney. Once on the city side, descend into The Rocks: Sydney's oldest neighbourhood, sandstone laneways, 200-year-old buildings turned restaurants, weekend markets, and rooftop bars with the Bridge as backdrop.

💡 Tip: Walk over in the morning for the best harbour light. The pedestrian path is on the eastern side of the Bridge. The Rocks Market runs Saturdays and Sundays.
Sun & Surf

The beaches that make
Sydney legendary.

Sydney's beaches are world-famous for good reason. Always swim between the red and yellow flags — no exceptions.

🏄
Bondi Beach
📍 Campbell Parade, Bondi🚌 Bus from Bondi Junction

The most famous beach in Australia — 2 km of golden sand, the iconic Icebergs saltwater pool, and a buzzing café promenade.

More than just a beach — it's a cultural institution. The famous Bondi Icebergs Pool sits perched on the southern clifftop, open to the public for a small fee. Visit midweek morning to feel like a local rather than a tourist.

💡 Tip: Breakfast at Porch and Parlour on the promenade before the crowds arrive. The Icebergs pool is open year-round.
⛵
Manly Beach
📍 N Steyne, Manly NSW🚢 30-min Manly Ferry from Circular Quay

Sydney's other great beach — relaxed village feel, and the Manly Ferry crossing is a bucket-list harbour experience in itself.

Slower, breezier, and more laid-back than Bondi. Two km of surf backed by Norfolk Island pines. The ferry crossing through the harbour past the Opera House costs no more than a bus fare and is unmissable.

💡 Tip: Take the ferry at sunset and look back at the Opera House and Harbour Bridge as you return. Use your Opal card.
🌿
Bronte Beach
📍 Bronte Rd, Bronte🚌 Bus from Bondi Junction (~10 min)

The insider pick — smaller, more sheltered, less crowded. The heritage Bronte Baths ocean pool is stunning year-round.

This is where Sydneysiders actually go. The Bronte Baths — a heritage ocean pool carved into the clifftop rock — lets you swim in calm water while waves crash alongside. A lush grassy park backs the beach for long, lazy afternoons.

💡 Tip: A natural stop on the Bondi-to-Coogee walk. The ocean baths are open year-round and are genuinely beautiful even in cooler months.
🤿
Clovelly Beach
📍 Clovelly Rd, Clovelly🚌 Bus from Bondi Junction

Not a surf beach — a sheltered ocean inlet with crystal-clear calm water, ideal for snorkelling year-round. The resident grouper fish are nearly tame.

The tame blue grouper fish that live in the bay have grown almost comfortable with swimmers — one of Sydney's great free wildlife moments. The calm, sheltered water makes this ideal even when the ocean is rough.

💡 Tip: Bring or hire a snorkel mask. A short detour from the Bondi-to-Coogee walk — absolutely worth the stop in any season.
🌅
Blues Point Reserve
📍 Blues Point Rd, North Sydney🚶 10-min walk from the office · Free, always open

Not a beach — but the best sunset spot in Sydney. Unobstructed 180° view of both the Harbour Bridge and Opera House in one frame.

A small harbourside park that delivers the photograph that defines Sydney. Free, always open, and magnificent in every season. After-work picnics here as the sun sets over the city are one of the great simple pleasures Sydney offers.

💡 Tip: Arrive 30 minutes before sunset. As the light turns gold, both the Bridge and Opera House glow simultaneously. Grab food and a drink from Miller St beforehand.
Coffee Culture

Australia invented the
flat white. Act accordingly.

Sydney's café culture is genuinely world-class. Always order a flat white first — strong espresso with silky micro-foamed milk, no froth.

🌱
The Grounds of Alexandria
📍 7A/2 Huntley St, Alexandria🕐 Daily from 7:00 AM

Sydney's most talked-about café — a converted industrial space with its own working garden, bakery, and in-house roastery. There's a resident pig named Kevin Bacon.

Fresh pastries baked on-site, beautiful food presentation, and coffee roasted in-house. It's as much a destination as a café. Weekends are very busy — visit on a weekday morning for the best experience.

💡 Tip: Don't skip whatever fresh pastry is on display. The garden is worth wandering even if you're just having coffee.
🥞
bills, Darlinghurst
📍 433 Liverpool St, Darlinghurst🕐 Daily 7:30 AM – 3:00 PM

Bill Granger's original café — credited with setting the global standard for Sydney brunch. The ricotta hotcakes are non-negotiable.

The ricotta hotcakes with honeycomb butter are among the most imitated dishes in Australian food history. Blonde wood, flowers, natural light. A genuine piece of Sydney food culture that set the brunch trend globally.

💡 Tip: Order the ricotta hotcakes. Non-negotiable. The scrambled eggs — impossibly creamy — are also a revelation.
🔬
ONA Coffee, Marrickville
📍 171 Addison Rd, Marrickville🕐 Weekdays 7 AM · Weekends 8 AM

The gold standard for specialty coffee in Sydney — run by Australia's World Barista Champion. A genuine pilgrimage spot for coffee people.

Every cup is an education in flavour, origin, and craft. The baristas love talking you through the seasonal filter options. Pair with the Moroccan eggs for the best breakfast combo in the city.

💡 Tip: Ask the barista about the seasonal single-origin filter. Inner West line train from the city. Absolutely worth the trip.
🫖
Brewmance, Crows Nest
📍 56 Willoughby Rd, Crows Nest🕐 Mon–Fri 6:30 AM–3 PM · Sat–Sun 7:30 AM–3 PM

Your closest excellent café to the office — 10 min walk, rated 4.9★. The Earl Grey Matcha latte is something you won't find elsewhere.

Dog-friendly, latte art taken seriously, and a creative menu. The Earl Grey Matcha latte is unlike anything else. Crows Nest village around it has excellent dinner options for after work.

💡 Tip: Your default morning coffee stop for the two weeks. Gets busy 8–9 AM — arrive slightly before or after the rush.
🥐
Flour & Stone, Woolloomooloo
📍 53 Riley St, Woolloomooloo🕐 Tue–Fri 7 AM–3 PM · Sat–Sun 8 AM–3 PM

One of Sydney's most beloved bakeries — cult status, genuinely earned. Everything sells out by 10 AM. The lamington is the signature.

Exceptional pastries: lamingtons, orange and almond cake, croissants made with extraordinary care. Consistently rated Sydney's best bakery by Broadsheet and Time Out. Worth queueing for.

💡 Tip: Arrive early — the best items are gone by 10 AM. The lamington is the signature. Short walk from Kings Cross station.
Can't-Miss Experiences

Things you'll still be
talking about at home.

Beyond the beach and the café — the experiences that make Sydney genuinely unforgettable.

🎭
Sydney Opera House
📍 Bennelong Point, Sydney🚢 10-min ferry from Milsons Point · Tours daily

One of the great buildings of the 20th century. Free to visit outside — but a guided tour or evening performance is truly unforgettable.

The story of Danish architect Jørn Utzon (who never saw the finished building), the acoustics of the concert hall, and the harbour views from the front forecourt are all remarkable. If possible, catch an evening performance — the experience of being inside at night changes how you understand the city.

💡 Tip: The most dramatic approach is by ferry from Milsons Point — 10 minutes, normal Opal fare. Arrive from the water for the full effect.
🦘
Taronga Zoo
📍 Bradleys Head Rd, Mosman🚢 12-min ferry from Circular Quay · Daily 9:30 AM–5 PM

World-class zoo above the harbour. Koalas, kangaroos, Tasmanian devils — wildlife that exists nowhere else. Book the Koala Encounter early.

Set on a hillside with the Sydney skyline as the literal backdrop for its animals. The Koala Encounter — five minutes up close with a koala — must be booked well in advance and sells out weeks ahead.

💡 Tip: Book the Koala Encounter the moment you land ($15 extra — it sells out). Take the cable car down from the zoo's upper entrance for the best harbour view.
🪨
The Rocks & Circular Quay
📍 The Rocks, Sydney NSW🚢 Ferry from Milsons Point, or walk across Harbour Bridge

Sydney's oldest neighbourhood — sandstone laneways, 200-year-old restaurants, weekend markets, and the best Harbour Bridge rooftop view in the city.

Built on the foundations of the original 1788 British colony. Today: the Rocks Market on weekends, fresh oysters at the quayside, and the free Museum of Contemporary Art hosting world-class exhibitions.

💡 Tip: The Glenmore Hotel rooftop bar has the best Harbour Bridge view of any bar in Sydney. The Rocks Market runs Saturdays and Sundays.
🌸
Royal Botanic Garden
📍 Mrs Macquaries Rd, Sydney · Free entry always🚶 10-min walk from Circular Quay

30 hectares on the harbour edge, always free. Walk to Mrs Macquaries Point for the iconic Opera House + Bridge shot. At dusk, thousands of flying foxes depart.

Walk through to Mrs Macquaries Point for the most-photographed view of the Opera House and Harbour Bridge side by side. At dusk, thousands of grey-headed flying foxes leave their roosts in the fig trees — a spectacular and surreal piece of Sydney wildlife right in the CBD.

💡 Tip: Visit at dusk to see the flying foxes depart. It's one of Sydney's great free wildlife experiences and most visitors have no idea it happens.
🎡
Luna Park Sydney
📍 1 Olympic Dr, Milsons Point · 5-min walk from the office🕐 Fri–Sun from 10 AM (hours vary — check website)

Sydney's iconic heritage amusement park right on the harbour — 5 minutes from the North Sydney office. The Bridge lit behind the rides at night is magical.

The giant laughing face entrance is one of the most photographed spots in the city. Rides, carnival games, and unbeatable Harbour Bridge views right from the park. Special themed events run throughout the year. At night with the Bridge illuminated behind the fairy lights, it's genuinely magical — and it's right next to your office.

💡 Tip: Visit at night for the best atmosphere — the Bridge lit up behind the rides is unforgettable. Check lunaparksydney.com for current event nights and ride passes.
Before You Step Out

Things every first-timer
needs to know.

🚆

Get an Opal Card First

Sydney's transport card covers all trains, buses, and ferries. Tap on and off every journey. Available at 7-Eleven, newsagencies, and the airport. A weekly cap limits total spend.

☀️

The UV is No Joke

Sydney's UV burns fair skin in under 15 minutes — even on cloudy days. SPF 50+, a hat, sunglasses — always. You will be outside more than you think.

🌊

Swim Between the Flags

Red and yellow flags mark the safe, patrolled sections. Sydney's beaches have rips and currents. Always swim between the flags, no exceptions.

☕

Order a Flat White

Not a latte. Not a cappuccino. A flat white — strong espresso with silky micro-foamed milk, no froth. Australia's gift to global coffee culture. Your first order in every café.

🍽️

Eat Like a Local

Try: fresh oysters, a meat pie, a prawn roll, Tim Tams (try the "Tim Tam slam"), and a lamington. Sydney's seafood is genuinely world-class.

🧥

Pack in Layers

Sydney days are warm, evenings cool down significantly. A light jacket is essential for evenings. A compact waterproof layer is also worth carrying.

🚢

Ferries are Underrated

The harbour ferry network is a real way to get around — the Manly Ferry from Circular Quay is a 30-minute harbour cruise that costs the same as a bus fare.

🦜

Expect Wildlife Everywhere

Cockatoos, kookaburras, rainbow lorikeets, ibis, and possums in parks. Don't feed them — especially ibis (locally called "bin chickens").

💬

Learn Some Aussie

"No worries" = you're welcome. "Mate" = friend (universal). "Arvo" = afternoon. "Brekkie" = breakfast. "Servo" = petrol station.
